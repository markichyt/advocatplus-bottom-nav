Legal Basis of This Profile

An overview of why this page exists and your rights as the named attorney.

1. What this page is (and is not)

This page is an informational attorney profile created from publicly available licensing records. Its primary purposes are:

  • to reflect basic, factual information about a licensed attorney (name, jurisdiction, license status, address of record, years licensed); and
  • to give the attorney an easy way to claim, correct, or remove this profile if they wish.

This is not an official bar website, is not an endorsement or rating, and does not imply that the attorney is affiliated with or recommends our platform.

2. Use of public bar records and copyright law

Most US state bar associations treat certain attorney licensing data as public records — for example: full name, jurisdiction(s) of admission, bar number, license status, address of record or city/state, and year of admission where published.

Under US copyright law, facts themselves are not copyrightable. The US Supreme Court confirmed this in Feist Publications, Inc. v. Rural Telephone Service Co., holding that names, towns and phone numbers in a directory were "nothing more than raw data" and therefore not protected by copyright.

3. First Amendment and attorney directories (Avvo case law)

US courts have held that attorney directories built from public information are a form of protected speech under the First Amendment. In Vrdolyak v. Avvo, Inc., the federal court (N.D. Illinois) dismissed a publicity-rights challenge, holding that Avvo's lawyer listings and ratings were non-commercial speech fully protected by the First Amendment, and that publishing truthful, newsworthy information about licensed attorneys — even together with ads — was constitutional.

4. No copying from private directories (hiQ v. LinkedIn)

In the hiQ Labs / LinkedIn litigation, the Ninth Circuit held that accessing public profiles was not, by itself, a violation of the Computer Fraud and Abuse Act (CFAA). However, when the case returned to the trial court, LinkedIn won on a breach of contract theory.

In light of this case law, our platform is designed to obtain attorney data only from official bar registries and other sources that permit such use.

5. Your rights: claim, correct, or remove this profile

Even though US law allows publication of truthful, publicly available information about licensed attorneys, we believe each lawyer should have practical control over how they appear here. For that reason, we provide two clear options on every profile:

  • Claim Profile. Verify that you are the attorney named on this page. Once verified, you can correct or update factual information, add additional professional details, upload your own photo and contact preferences, and manage how (and whether) this profile is displayed.
  • Request Removal. If you do not wish to appear in this directory at all, you may request removal.
6. Important disclaimer

This explanation is provided for informational purposes only and is based on publicly available case law and commentary regarding US copyright, First Amendment, and publicity-rights doctrine. Laws and bar rules can vary by state and may change over time. Nothing on this page is intended to be, or should be taken as, individualized legal advice to any attorney or client.

References: Feist Publications, Inc. v. Rural Telephone Service Co., 499 U.S. 340 (1991); Vrdolyak v. Avvo, Inc., 206 F. Supp. 3d 1384 (N.D. Ill. 2016); the hiQ Labs / LinkedIn decisions on public data access, CFAA, and breach of contract.
